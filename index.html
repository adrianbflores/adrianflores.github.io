About Me
The work I find
most interesting

I grew up in a first-generation household where figuring things out without a manual was the baseline expectation. Nobody handed you a playbook. You assessed the situation, built what you needed, and got it done. That instinct followed me into my career and it is the reason I keep ending up in roles where the problems are undefined, the ownership is unclear, and someone needs to step in and make something out of nothing.

At PIMCO I serve as one of the leads on the Corporate Actions desk and as the business-side project lead for a net-new proprietary platform build. On any given day that means coordinating between Portfolio Managers, C-suite leadership, Legal, IT, external vendors, and the data warehouse team — groups that do not share the same priorities, vocabulary, or definitions of success. In the past year I co-launched the firm's first enterprise BI reporting platform, deployed an AI classification bot running at 98% accuracy, built the institutional knowledge infrastructure for the entire department, and drove 35+ initiatives across five simultaneous workflows. None of those were in the original job description.

Outside of work I built and run a personal finance coaching practice that started during COVID-19 when people around me were losing jobs and needed someone to help them think through the financial side without judgment. First-generation college graduate. Dean's List at UNLV with a 3.75 GPA. Currently pursuing CFA Level I. I am actively looking for senior operations and program management roles at companies that move fast, build hard things, and hold people accountable for outcomes rather than activity. If that describes your team, I would like to talk.
